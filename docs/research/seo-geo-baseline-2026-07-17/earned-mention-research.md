## Qualification record required before any contact

For every candidate record:

- exact target URL and publisher identity;
- audience and topical fit;
- dated editorial/contribution policy URL;
- commercial-disclosure and link rules;
- content and image rights;
- whether an existing resource gap can be filled without duplicating the publisher's content;
- which getgiffgaff claim/source package is ready;
- identity/trademark, release and claim-freshness gate results;
- manual reviewer decision.

A public email address, contact form or open GitHub repository is not permission to pitch or place a promotional link.

## Prohibited tactics

- paid or reciprocal link placement;
- specified anchor text, `dofollow` requests or rank guarantees;
- mass email, automated DMs, duplicate platform posts or forum flooding;
- GitHub Issues/PRs created primarily to manufacture backlinks;
- fabricated customer stories, metrics, citations or author credentials;
- official, authorized, partnership or endorsement implications;
- hiding the site's commercial relationship where disclosure is required.

## Outcome ledger

Keep the following distinct:

- `candidate`: a researched publisher or community; not a backlink.
- `permission-fit`: public policy appears compatible; not target-specific approval.
- `contacted`: a real authorized message was sent and retained.
- `editor-accepted`: the target explicitly accepted the contribution.
- `brand-mention-live`: an independently edited, verified referring page mentions getgiffgaff.
- `backlink-live`: the verified referring page contains a clickable link.

Only an exact, independently reopened referring URL may count as a live mention or backlink. Record date, context, disclosure and whether the publisher selected the wording independently. Never convert candidate counts into referring-domain totals.
